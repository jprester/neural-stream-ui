Google DeepMind released Gemma 4 on April 2, 2026 — a family of four open models built from the same research as Gemini 3, and for the first time released under a fully permissive Apache 2.0 license enabling unrestricted commercial use, modification, and redistribution.

The family spans from a compact edge model up to a 31B parameter flagship, all supporting multimodal inputs (text and images across all sizes, with audio on edge variants), 256K-token context windows, and native reasoning and agentic workflow capabilities. The 31B model saw dramatic benchmark improvements — AIME math jumped from 20.8% to 89.2% — rivalling much larger closed models.

Gemma models have been downloaded over 400 million times since the first generation launched, with a community of more than 100,000 model variants built on top. The Apache 2.0 shift marks a significant policy change from Google, making Gemma 4 one of the most commercially open frontier-class models available.

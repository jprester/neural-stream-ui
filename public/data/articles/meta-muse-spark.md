Meta debuted Muse Spark on April 8, 2026 — the first model from its newly formed Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL), led by Chief AI Officer Alexandr Wang. Codenamed Avocado internally, Muse Spark is a natively multimodal reasoning model supporting visual chain-of-thought, tool use, and multi-agent orchestration, developed with input from over 1,000 physicians for health-related query handling.

The release marks a decisive break from Meta's Llama open-source tradition. Muse Spark is a closed, proprietary model — a major strategic shift driven by the company's $115–135 billion AI capital expenditure plan for 2026, nearly double the prior year. Muse Spark now powers the Meta AI app, WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook.

The model's arrival comes nine months after Meta hired Alexandr Wang, founder of Scale AI, to lead its AI efforts. Muse Spark is positioned to compete directly with GPT-5.4 and Gemini 3.1 Pro on general reasoning and multimodal tasks, signalling Meta's intent to compete at the frontier rather than cede that ground to open-source derivatives.

OpenAI released GPT-5.4 on March 5, 2026, available in Thinking and Pro variants, with mini and nano versions following on March 17. It is the first general-purpose frontier model with native, state-of-the-art computer-use capabilities, scoring 75% on the OSWorld-Verified benchmark against a human average of 72.4%.

GPT-5.4 supports a 1M-token context window and consolidates coding, reasoning, and agentic workflow capabilities into a single model. It is 33% less likely to make factual errors than its predecessor GPT-5.2, and achieved a record 83% on OpenAI's GDPval benchmark for knowledge work tasks.

The release cements computer use as a standard capability for frontier models — following Anthropic's earlier computer-use tooling in Claude — and sets a new bar for how AI systems interact with desktop software, browsers, and operating system interfaces autonomously.

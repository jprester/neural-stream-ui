Anthropic announced on April 7, 2026 that its most capable model to date — Claude Mythos — will not receive a general public release. Instead, the model is being made available exclusively to a curated consortium of more than 40 technology and cybersecurity companies under a restricted access program called Project Glasswing.

The model's existence became public after a security researcher discovered approximately 3,000 unpublished internal assets in an unprotected Anthropic database on March 28, including draft blog posts describing Mythos. Anthropic patched the exposure the same night. Internal evaluations found the model to be exceptionally capable at cybersecurity tasks, surfacing vulnerabilities in critical software at a level that prompted Anthropic to withhold general access.

Project Glasswing allows vetted partners to work with Claude Mythos specifically to improve the security of widely-used software. The decision reflects Anthropic's stated policy of gating the most powerful model variants when potential misuse risk outweighs public benefit — the first time the company has declined to ship a frontier model to the public entirely.
